Most effective treatments

These are the treatments I would feature most prominently because they have the strongest clinical support and are recommended in major guidelines.

Highly recommended

EMDR

Best fit when traumatic memories, body sensations, images, shame, fear, or stuck emotional charge feel hard to process through ordinary talk therapy.

Likely improvement statistics

These numbers are broad estimates from research summaries and trials. Real outcomes depend on trauma complexity, therapist skill, safety, dissociation, substance use, sleep, social support, and treatment completion.

53%

No longer meet PTSD criteria after top therapies

VA research summarizes that about 53 of 100 people receiving one of three leading psychotherapies no longer have PTSD afterward.

Source: VA Research PTSD summary.
40%

No longer have PTSD after CPT in a veteran RCT

In a VA-described randomized trial with U.S. Veterans, 40% receiving CPT no longer had PTSD by the end of treatment.

Source: VA CPT overview.
83%

No longer met criteria at long-term PE follow-up

VA’s PE overview cites long-term follow-up data where 83% receiving PE no longer met diagnostic criteria six years later.

Source: VA PE overview.
76.5%

Completed WET in one service-member trial

In a JAMA Network Open trial, 76.5% completed Written Exposure Therapy versus 54.8% completing CPT.

Source: JAMA Network Open WET vs CPT trial.

Plain-language takeaway

For many people, evidence-based PTSD therapy produces major symptom reduction in weeks to months. The best practical expectation is meaningful improvement, less avoidance, fewer symptoms, more choice, and a real chance of no longer meeting PTSD criteria.

EMDR best practices

EMDR works best as a structured therapy, not as random bilateral stimulation. The structure matters.

Best practice

Use the 8-phase protocol

The standard EMDR map is: history and treatment planning, preparation, assessment, desensitization, installation, body scan, closure, and reevaluation.

EMDRIA: 8 phases →
Best practice

Resource before processing

Preparation should include stabilization, grounding, containment, safe-place or calm-place work, and agreement about stopping or slowing the process.

APA: EMDR phases →
Best practice

Track SUD and VOC

Good EMDR tracks Subjective Units of Distress and Validity of Cognition so sessions are guided by measurable change, not therapist intuition alone.

Best EMDR kit setup

Ideal: wireless light tube + wireless tactile pulsators + headphones/audio. Why: visual stimulation is traditional and powerful, tactile stimulation can be gentler and easier with eyes closed, and audio can support remote or self-regulation work. A full kit lets the therapist adjust speed, intensity, distance, modality, and session pacing.

Self-assessed techniques that often help

These are simple, low-friction regulation practices. They are best used for state-shifting, grounding, and coming back into the room.

↕

Jump and shake for 20 seconds

Jump up and down lightly, then shake loosely through the shoulders, arms, hips, knees, and jaw. Let the body discharge activation without trying to think it away.

◎

Take in the whole room

Open peripheral vision. Let the whole room or outdoor space come into awareness. Then name or focus on five specific objects.

4:6

Breathe in 4, out 6

Inhale for 4 seconds and exhale for 6 seconds. Repeat for 2–5 minutes. The longer exhale supports downshifting.

Why the page focuses only on highly recommended resources

People with PTSD are already carrying too much. This page favors high-signal options: major guidelines, validated measures, proven therapies, practical self-regulation, and clear educational videos.

How to use this page with a therapist

Bring your PCL-5 or ITQ scores, choose one or two therapies from the decision aid, and ask the therapist what formal training and supervision they have in that exact method.

How to use EMDR equipment

Professional EMDR equipment is best used inside an 8-phase EMDR therapy process. For personal regulation, use gentle bilateral audio, butterfly tapping, orienting, breathing, and movement without trying to process major trauma alone.
